# Stance Auto Magazine — Article Correction & Enhancement

## When To Use This Skill
Use this skill when correcting and enhancing submitted articles from contributors for Stance Auto Magazine. The priority is always preserving the author's voice — this is an edit, not a rewrite.

---

## Objective
Correct spelling and grammar in submitted articles while preserving the original content, voice, and tone of the author. The goal is to maintain the personal storytelling aspect of each piece. Contributors share their own experiences — that authenticity is what makes the content relatable to Stance Auto readers.

After correcting, rough up the writing slightly. Loosen sentences, drop unnecessary commas, make it sound like someone who knows their stuff but isn't trying to impress an English teacher. The human touch must remain intact.

**Output format:** Google Docs-friendly. Clean, copy-paste ready. No special formatting that breaks on paste.

---

## What To Correct
- Spelling errors
- Grammar mistakes
- Punctuation issues
- Sentence structure where it genuinely obscures meaning

## What To Preserve
- The author's personal voice and tone
- Their storytelling style and narrative flow
- Colloquialisms or informal phrasing that adds character
- Their perspective and opinions — do not editorialise
- The human, authentic feel of the piece

---

## SEO Block (Always Output In This Order Before The Article)

**Slug** — Max 60 characters, lowercase, hyphens only, focus keyword included.

**Meta Description** — 180 characters max. Include the main keyword. Compelling and click-worthy.

**Keywords** — 15 total, relevant to the car, its build, and modifications. Mix of short-tail and long-tail, comma-separated.

**Focus Keyword** — Single phrase. Must appear in: SEO title, meta description, slug, first 10% of body, at least one H2 or H3, and the image alt text. Maintain a natural keyword density above 0.60% throughout the article.

**Image Alt Text** — Descriptive, includes focus keyword.

---

## Article Structure

**Title Format:** [Focus Keyword] — high search-intent H1, max 60 characters. Reflect common user queries using technical terms, car model identifiers, or popular phrases.

**Headings:** H2, H3, H4 hierarchy. Include the article's main keyword in headings. Guide the reader logically through the content.

**Body:** Preserve the author's paragraph structure where possible. Bold high-authority keywords contextually within the body — not just in headings. No bullet points.

**Backlinks:** Include at least one internal backlink to a relevant article on [https://stanceauto.co.uk](https://stanceauto.co.uk) within the body text.

**External DoFollow Link:** Include one external DoFollow link relevant to the car, brand, or modification featured in the article.
